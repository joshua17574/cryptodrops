How Airdrops Work

‚ñº

Understanding blockchain snapshots, eligibility criteria, and token distribution mechanisms. (Click to learn more)

üì∏ The Snapshot:
Projects take a "snapshot" of the blockchain at a random time to see who qualifies. This records your wallet activity, balances, and transactions.

‚úÖ How to Qualify:

  • Use the platform regularly (make transactions, swap tokens, etc.)
  • Hold certain tokens or NFTs
  • Provide liquidity to exchanges
  • Participate in community voting

üéÅ How You Get Tokens:

  • Automatic: Tokens just appear in your wallet (rare)
  • Manual Claim: Visit the project's website and claim your tokens (most common)

üí° Important: Gas fees for claiming can be high on Ethereum ($20-100), but cheap on Layer 2 networks like Arbitrum (under $1).

Airdrop Safety Tips

Essential security practices to protect yourself from scams

‚ö†Ô∏è

Never Share Private Keys

‚ñº

Your private keys and seed phrase are the ONLY way to access your funds. No legitimate airdrop will ever ask for them.

  • Never enter your seed phrase on any website or form
  • Don't share screenshots of your wallet recovery phrase
  • Be wary of fake customer support asking for wallet access
  • Keep your seed phrase offline and securely stored
  • If someone asks for your private keys, it's 100% a scam
üîó

Verify Official Links

‚ñº

Scammers create fake websites that look identical to real projects. Always double-check URLs before connecting your wallet.

  • Verify URLs character by character (watch for subtle misspellings)
  • Only use links from official Twitter, Discord, or Telegram accounts
  • Check for HTTPS and security certificates
  • Bookmark official websites for quick access
  • Be suspicious of links sent via DM - official teams rarely DM first
  • Common scam tactics: replacing "o" with "0", adding extra letters, or using different domains (.com vs .io)
üí∞

No Upfront Payments

‚ñº

Real airdrops are completely free. If you're asked to pay anything, it's a scam designed to steal your money.

  • Never send ETH, USDT, or any crypto to "verify" or "activate" an airdrop
  • Gas fees are normal, but sending tokens to claim is NOT
  • Ignore messages saying you need to "pay a small fee to unlock rewards"
  • Be wary of "VIP access" or "priority claiming" for a fee
  • Scammers may claim fees go to "smart contract activation" - this is false
‚úÖ

Use Separate Wallets

‚ñº

Protect your main holdings by using dedicated wallets for airdrop farming. This limits potential damage from malicious contracts.

  • Create a new wallet specifically for airdrop activities
  • Never keep large amounts in your airdrop wallet
  • Transfer earned tokens to your main secure wallet after claiming
  • Use hardware wallets (Ledger, Trezor) for storing valuable assets
  • Consider using different wallets for different chains
  • Regularly revoke token approvals from old contracts
üõ°Ô∏è

Research Before Participating

‚ñº

Not all airdrops are legitimate. Do your homework before connecting your wallet or completing tasks.

  • Check project social media for verification badges
  • Read community feedback and reviews from trusted sources
  • Verify the project team members and their credentials
  • Look for red flags: anonymous teams, no whitepaper, unrealistic promises
  • Check if the project has real utility and a working product
  • Be skeptical of airdrops promising extremely high rewards
üîç

Review Smart Contract Permissions

‚ñº

When connecting your wallet, pay attention to what permissions you're granting. Malicious contracts can drain your funds.

  • Read transaction details carefully before signing
  • Use tools like Revoke.cash to check and remove old approvals
  • Never approve unlimited spending limits if you can avoid it
  • Understand what "Set Approval for All" means before accepting
  • If a transaction looks suspicious or unclear, reject it
  • Keep track of all contracts you've interacted with
üö®

Common Red Flags to Watch For

‚ñº

Recognize these warning signs to avoid scams and protect your assets.

  • Urgent deadlines or pressure to act immediately
  • Guaranteed returns or "get rich quick" promises
  • Poor grammar and spelling in official communications
  • Requests to download unknown software or extensions
  • Unsolicited DMs claiming you've won an airdrop
  • Projects with no clear roadmap or use case
  • Websites with unprofessional design or broken links
  • No community presence or fake follower counts

Best Practices for Safe Airdrop Participation

‚ñº

Follow these guidelines to maximize your safety while participating in airdrops.

  • Only participate in airdrops listed on reputable platforms
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all accounts
  • Use a password manager for strong, unique passwords
  • Keep your wallet software and browser extensions updated
  • Never share wallet addresses publicly unless necessary
  • Be cautious about KYC requirements - only provide to trusted projects
  • Join official communities to stay updated on legitimate airdrops
  • Trust your instincts - if something feels wrong, stay away
